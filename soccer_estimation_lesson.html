📚 Key Vocabulary

🎯 Estimation: Finding an approximate value that is close to the actual answer
📊 Approximation: A value that is not exact but close enough for practical purposes
🔄 Rounding: Adjusting a number to the nearest specified place value
⭐ Benchmark Numbers: Easy-to-use reference numbers (like 10, 100, 1000)

🎯 Guided Examples with Solutions

Example 1: 🏟️ The San Siro Stadium

Problem: The San Siro stadium, where Maldini played for 25 years, has a capacity of 80,018 fans. If tickets cost €47 each, estimate the total revenue from a sold-out match. 💰

Solution Steps:
Step 1: Round capacity → 80,018 ≈ 80,000
Step 2: Round ticket price → €47 ≈ €50
Step 3: Calculate → 80,000 × 50 = €4,000,000

✅ Actual calculation: 80,018 × 47 = €3,760,846

Our estimate of €4,000,000 is very reasonable! 🎯

Example 2: ⚽ Soccer Strategy - Defensive Positioning

Problem: During a corner kick, Maldini positions himself 9.7 meters from the goal line and 6.2 meters from the corner flag. Estimate his distance from the corner of the goal using the Pythagorean theorem. 📐

Solution Steps:
Step 1: Round distances → 9.7 ≈ 10m, 6.2 ≈ 6m
Step 2: Apply Pythagorean theorem → d² = 10² + 6²
Step 3: Calculate → d² = 100 + 36 = 136
Step 4: Estimate → d ≈ √144 = 12 meters

✅ Actual calculation: √(9.7² + 6.2²) = √132.53 ≈ 11.5 meters

Our estimate of 12 meters is excellent! 🎯

Example 3: 🏃‍♀️ Young Sofia's Practice

Problem: Sofia, inspired by Maldini's precision, practices estimating angles during her youth soccer training. If she runs from one corner of a rectangular field (108m × 68m) to the opposite corner, estimate the angle her path makes with the longer sideline. 📏

Solution Steps:
Step 1: Round dimensions → 108m ≈ 110m, 68m ≈ 70m
Step 2: Use tangent ratio → tan(θ) = 70/110 = 7/11 ≈ 0.64
Step 3: Estimate angle → Since tan(30°) ≈ 0.58, our angle is slightly larger
Estimate: About 32°

⚽ Problem Set A: Basic Estimation

Problem 1 🚀
A powerful soccer kick travels at 118 km/h. Estimate how many meters it travels in 0.5 seconds.

Solution: Round speed: 118 km/h ≈ 120 km/h

Convert to m/s: 120 km/h = 120,000m ÷ 3600s = 33.3 m/s ≈ 33 m/s

Distance = 33 m/s × 0.5s = 16.5 meters ⚽

Problem 2 🏆
The UEFA Champions League trophy weighs 7.5 kg. If 15 trophies were made for display, estimate the total weight.

Solution: 7.5 × 15 ≈ 8 × 15 = 120 kg 🏆

Problem 3 📊
Sofia's team scored these goals in their last 6 games: 2, 4, 1, 3, 5, 2. Estimate their average goals per game.

Solution: Total goals: 2 + 4 + 1 + 3 + 5 + 2 = 17

Average: 17 ÷ 6 ≈ 18 ÷ 6 = 3 goals per game ⚽

Quick tip: Notice the numbers cluster around 3! 🎯

🏃‍♂️ Problem Set B: Intermediate Applications

Problem 4 🏟️
During the 1994 World Cup, Maldini's team played in stadiums with these capacities: 94,348; 77,622; 83,091; 68,237. Estimate the total capacity of all four stadiums by rounding each to the nearest thousand.

Solution: Round each capacity:

94,348 ≈ 94,000 | 77,622 ≈ 78,000

83,091 ≈ 83,000 | 68,237 ≈ 68,000

Total: 94,000 + 78,000 + 83,000 + 68,000 = 323,000 🏟️

Problem 5 📏
A soccer field is 105.2 meters long and 67.8 meters wide. Estimate:
a) The perimeter of the field
b) The area of the field

Solutions:

a) Perimeter: 2(105.2 + 67.8) ≈ 2(105 + 68) = 2(173) = 346 meters

b) Area: 105.2 × 67.8 ≈ 105 × 70 = 7,350 square meters ⚽

🏆 Problem Set C: Advanced Challenge

Problem 7 🎯
Maldini played in 647 Serie A games. His team won approximately 55% of them. Estimate the total number of games his team won.

Solution: Round games to 650 and 55% to 50%

Wins ≈ 50% of 650 = 325

Since 55% > 50%, estimate slightly higher: 350-360 games 🏆

Problem 8 💰
Sofia's team is buying new equipment. Estimate the total cost for:
• 17 jerseys at €28.99 each
• 17 pairs of shorts at €14.50 each
• 8 soccer balls at €21.25 each

Solution:

Jerseys: 17 × €28.99 ≈ 17 × €30 = €510

Shorts: 17 × €14.50 ≈ 17 × €15 = €255

Balls: 8 × €21.25 ≈ 8 × €20 = €160

Total: €510 + €255 + €160 = €925 💰

Problem 9 📹
A camera is mounted on top of a 15.8m tall pole at the corner of a 105m by 68m soccer field. Estimate the straight-line distance from the camera to the center of the field.

Solution:

Step 1: Ground distance to center ≈ √(50² + 35²) = √(2500 + 1225) ≈ √3600 = 60m

Step 2: Total distance ≈ √(60² + 16²) = √(3600 + 256) ≈ √3856 ≈ 62m 📹
